# UIUC AI Education Landscape: Comprehensive Research Report

**Prepared for**: Campus AI Curriculum Task Force
**Date**: November 23, 2025
**Research Focus**: Institutional analysis of AI education programs, interdisciplinary models, faculty expertise, and infrastructure at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign

---

## Executive Summary

The University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign (UIUC) has established itself as a national leader in AI education with a top-five ranked AI program, nearly $270 million in funding for AI projects and initiatives since 2019, and innovative interdisciplinary program models that serve as national exemplars.

### Key Findings:

1. **Extensive AI Course Offerings**: UIUC offers comprehensive AI education across multiple departments including Computer Science (CS 440, CS 446, CS 447, CS 547), Electrical and Computer Engineering (ECE 448, ECE 449, ECE 549), Statistics (STAT 432), and Information Sciences (IS 327, IS 557).

2. **Proven Interdisciplinary Models**:
   - **CS+X Programs**: 14 blended CS programs across 5 colleges with 1,098 students enrolled (as of Fall 2022)
   - **X+DS Programs**: 4 data science programs launched Fall 2023 with 850 students from diverse disciplines
   - Both models demonstrate successful approaches to interdisciplinary education with strong enrollment and growth

3. **World-Class Faculty Expertise**: Faculty spanning computer science, ECE, statistics, and information sciences lead research in machine learning, computer vision, natural language processing, and AI applications across multiple domains.

4. **Robust AI Infrastructure**: Multiple research institutes (IBM-Illinois Discovery Accelerator, C3.ai Digital Transformation Institute, Molecule Maker Lab), advanced computing resources (DeltaAI, HOLL-I, Delta), and industry partnerships (IBM, Microsoft, Amazon, Google, Capital One).

5. **Multiple Program Modalities**: Undergraduate degrees, graduate programs, professional certificates, online programs (MCS-DS), and K-12 outreach initiatives create a comprehensive AI education ecosystem.

## II. CS+X Program Analysis

### A. Program Structure

**Overview:**
- 14 "blended" CS programs across 5 colleges
- University approved the CS+X interdisciplinary programs in 2013
- First freshmen matriculated in Fall 2014
- Template created in 2014 for "computer science plus LAS option"
- As of Fall 2022: 1,098 students enrolled across CS+X programs vs. 1,296 in straight CS program

**Current CS+X Concentrations** (spanning 5 colleges):

*College of Liberal Arts & Sciences (LAS):*
- CS + Anthropology
- CS + Astronomy
- CS + Chemistry
- CS + Economics (highest enrollment: 156 students as of Fall 2022)
- CS + Geography and Geographic Information Science
- CS + Linguistics
- CS + Philosophy

*College of Agricultural, Consumer & Environmental Sciences (ACES):*
- CS + Animal Sciences
- CS + Crop Sciences

*College of Education:*
- CS + Education

*College of Fine and Applied Arts:*
- CS + Music

*College of Media:*
- CS + Advertising

*Grainger College of Engineering:*
- CS + Bioengineering (began Fall 2023)
- CS + Physics (began Fall 2023)

Program websites: [Blended CS Degrees](https://siebelschool.illinois.edu/academics/undergraduate/degree-program-options/cs-x-degree-programs), [CS+X Expansion News](https://siebelschool.illinois.edu/news/groundbreaking-cs-x-degrees-expand-into-bioengineering-and-physics)

### B. Curriculum Design

**Core Philosophy:**
- Students get a strong foundation in computer science that isn't a "watered down degree"
- Regular CS students take upper-level electives in different CS specialties
- CS+X students take advanced courses in their "X" field instead of CS electives
- CS+X degrees require fewer technical prerequisites than straight CS

**Example: CS+Anthropology Requirements**
- 8 courses from Anthropology
- 9-10 courses from Computer Science
- Balanced integration of both disciplines

**Key Features:**
- Core CS offerings run through Grainger College of Engineering
- Collaboration with faculty across UIUC colleges
- True interdisciplinary exploration at the intersection of CS and other academic fields
- Multi-college governance structure maintains rigorous standards in both disciplines

### C. Governance Model

**Approval Process:**
- Proposals submitted through Curriculum Inventory Management (CIM) system
- When proposal impacts other units' resources, Senate Committee on Educational Policy (EP) requires written confirmation
- Proposals approved at unit and college levels, then sent to Office of the Provost
- Interdisciplinary curriculum committees manage ongoing curriculum decisions

**Administrative Structure:**
- Multi-college collaboration model
- Each program involves partnership between:
  - Siebel School of Computing and Data Science
  - Partner college/department hosting the "X" discipline
- Shared governance and resource allocation

### D. Lessons Learned & Outcomes

**Challenges Identified:**

1. **Program Design Challenge**: The department hosting X must have clear understanding of what CS+X program would look like and, critically, **why it should exist**. Articulating the value proposition is essential.

2. **Curriculum Negotiation**: Each participating department must be prepared to **reduce their requirements** to create space for the interdisciplinary blend.

3. **Perception Management**: As novel programs, CS+X fields face scrutiny, rumors, and misinformation. Need active communication to address misconceptions.

**Success Factors:**

1. **Academic Rigor Maintained**: Difficulty level between CS and CS+X is nearly identical. Both populations learn the same fundamental computing principles, with tailored applications for CS+X students.

2. **Career Preparation**: CS majors and CS+X students take the same fundamental CS courses, making both equally equipped for technology careers (data analysts, software engineers, etc.).

3. **Strong Enrollment Growth**: Programs have grown from 4 initial offerings in 2014 to 14 programs by 2023, with 1,098 students enrolled.

4. **Selectivity**: CS+X acceptance rate of 18.5% (vs. traditional CS <7%) demonstrates competitiveness while providing alternative pathway.

5. **Inspired Further Innovation**: Success of CS+X programs directly inspired creation of X+DS programs.

**Student Outcomes:**
- Graduates work across technology sector
- Equal preparation for computing careers compared to traditional CS majors
- Unique interdisciplinary expertise valued by employers
- Strong placement in data science, software engineering, and domain-specific technical roles

**Key Recommendations from CS+X Experience:**

1. **Start with Clear Value Proposition**: Articulate why the interdisciplinary combination matters before designing curriculum
2. **Expect Curriculum Negotiation**: Both departments must compromise on requirements
3. **Invest in Communication**: Proactively address misconceptions about program rigor and outcomes
4. **Maintain Shared Core**: Ensure fundamental CS education matches traditional program
5. **Support Multi-College Governance**: Create clear administrative structures for collaboration
6. **Monitor Enrollment Trends**: Track student interest to identify successful combinations

## III. X+DS Program Analysis

### A. Program Structure

**Overview:**
- Launched Fall 2023 (applications accepted for Fall 2023 admissions cycle)
- Series of interdisciplinary undergraduate degrees in Data Science
- "X" represents existing degree program blended with DS
- Approved by Illinois Board of Higher Education
- Total enrollment: 850 students across all X+DS programs
- Inspired by success of CS+X model

**Current X+DS Programs (4 total):**

*Gies College of Business:*
- Accountancy + Data Science, BS
- Finance + Data Science, BS

*College of Liberal Arts & Sciences:*
- Astronomy + Data Science, BS

*School of Information Sciences:*
- Information Sciences + Data Science, BS

Program websites: [Blended DS Degrees](https://datascience.illinois.edu/), [X+DS Launch Announcement](https://siebelschool.illinois.edu/news/university-of-illinois-launches-x-ds-a-series-of-interdisciplinary-undergraduate-degrees-in-data-science), [Strategic Plan Impact Story](https://impact.strategicplan.illinois.edu/x-data-science-programs-support-interdisciplinary-learning/)

### B. Curriculum Design

**Core Data Science Coursework (~30 credit hours):**
- Designed to be completed within first 3-5 semesters
- Taught in partnership between:
  - Illinois Computer Science (Siebel School)
  - Department of Statistics
  - School of Information Sciences

**Five Core Components:**
1. Mathematical Foundations (calculus, linear algebra)
2. Data Science Fundamentals (STAT 107, STAT 207, CS 307)
3. Computational Fundamentals
4. Social Impact in Data Science
5. Research or Discovery Experience

**First Data Science Courses:**
- STAT 107 - foundation course
- STAT 207 - foundation course
- CS 307 - third core course

**Prerequisites:**
- One calculus course (e.g., MATH 234, MATH 220)
- One linear algebra course (e.g., MATH 227, MATH 257)
- Required prior to certain advanced courses

**Design Philosophy (Key Differentiator):**
- **Fewer technical prerequisites** than programs in computer science, mathematics, or statistics
- Making X+DS **more accessible to learners from all backgrounds**
- Designed "from the ground up to be interdisciplinary and inclusive"
- Approximately 30 credit hours DS core + X discipline requirements

**Example: Finance + Data Science Requirements**
- Learning objectives and assessment follow current process for undergraduate Finance students
- Careful monitoring of student progress through initial years
- Mentoring provided through advising process
- Blend of finance coursework with 30-hour DS core

### C. Governance Model

**Partnership Structure:**
- Three primary academic units collaborate:
  - Siebel School of Computing and Data Science
  - Department of Statistics
  - School of Information Sciences
- Partner colleges host the "X" component:
  - Gies College of Business (Accountancy+DS, Finance+DS)
  - College of Liberal Arts & Sciences (Astronomy+DS)
  - iSchool (Information Sciences+DS)

**Administrative Approach:**
- Each X+DS program maintains governance of X discipline
- Data science core jointly managed by CS, Statistics, iSchool
- Interdisciplinary curriculum committees oversee program development

### D. Assessment & Student Success

**Student Success Monitoring:**
- Careful monitoring of student progress through initial years
- Active advising and mentoring process
- Finance+DS: follows current assessment process for all undergraduate Finance students
- Career services provide coaching, career fairs, employer site visits, counseling, internship opportunities

**Career Preparation:**
- U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics projects **36% increase** in data science jobs by 2031
- Graduates work in: finance, insurance, information technology, professional/scientific/technical services
- Strong industry demand for data science skills across sectors

**Program Accessibility:**
- Lower barriers to entry compared to traditional CS/Statistics/Math programs
- Designed to welcome students from diverse academic backgrounds
- Emphasis on interdisciplinary thinking from day one

### E. Lessons Learned & Outcomes

**Strengths of X+DS Model:**

1. **Accessibility Focus**: Intentionally designed with fewer prerequisites to broaden participation
2. **Three-Unit Partnership**: Collaboration between CS, Statistics, and iSchool creates comprehensive data science foundation
3. **Rapid Growth**: 850 students across 4 programs in first 2 years shows strong demand
4. **Career Alignment**: Programs align with explosive growth in data science job market

**Challenges & Considerations:**

1. **Program Maturity**: Launched 2023, so long-term outcome data not yet available
2. **Assessment Development**: Programs still developing comprehensive outcome metrics
3. **Curriculum Coordination**: Three-unit partnership requires careful coordination
4. **Resource Allocation**: Managing faculty resources across multiple partner units

**Comparison to CS+X:**

| Aspect | CS+X | X+DS |
|--------|------|------|
| **Launch Year** | 2014 | 2023 |
| **Number of Programs** | 14 programs | 4 programs |
| **Enrollment** | 1,098 students (Fall 2022) | 850 students |
| **Colleges Involved** | 5 colleges | 3 colleges/units |
| **Prerequisites** | Standard CS prerequisites | Fewer technical prerequisites |
| **Core Partnership** | CS + Partner Department | CS + Statistics + iSchool + Partner |
| **Accessibility Focus** | Competitive, specialized | Intentionally inclusive |
| **Acceptance Rate** | 18.5% | Data not available |
| **Maturity** | 9+ years of data | <2 years of data |

**Key Recommendations from X+DS Experience:**

1. **Prioritize Accessibility**: Design curriculum with explicit goal of broadening participation
2. **Multi-Unit Collaboration**: Data science requires Statistics, CS, and domain expertise - plan for three-way partnership
3. **Front-Load Core**: Complete DS fundamentals in first 3-5 semesters to enable advanced work
4. **Career Services Integration**: Strong career support needed given rapid evolution of data science field
5. **Monitor Market Trends**: 36% job growth projection makes X+DS timely and relevant
6. **Build Assessment Infrastructure**: Develop comprehensive student success metrics early

**Comparison Insights:**
- CS+X model: 9 years of proven success, broader range of disciplines, established outcomes
- X+DS model: Newer but learning from CS+X, intentionally more accessible, aligned with job market growth
- Both models: Multi-college governance, maintain disciplinary rigor, create unique career pathways

Sources: [X+DS Launch](https://siebelschool.illinois.edu/news/university-of-illinois-launches-x-ds-a-series-of-interdisciplinary-undergraduate-degrees-in-data-science), [Data Science Catalog](https://catalog.illinois.edu/undergraduate/las/minors/data-science/), [Finance+DS Program](https://catalog.illinois.edu/undergraduate/bus/finance-data-science-bs/), [Information Sciences+DS](https://catalog.illinois.edu/undergraduate/ischool/information-sciences-data-science-bs/), [Strategic Plan Story](https://impact.strategicplan.illinois.edu/x-data-science-programs-support-interdisciplinary-learning/)

## IX. Lessons Learned & Recommendations

### A. Key Lessons from CS+X and X+DS Programs

#### Program Design Lessons

**1. Start with "Why"**
- Departments must clearly articulate why the interdisciplinary combination should exist
- Value proposition must be compelling beyond "CS is useful for X"
- Successful programs address genuine disciplinary intersections

**2. Prepare for Curriculum Negotiation**
- Both departments must reduce their requirements
- Compromise is essential for creating balanced interdisciplinary program
- Flexibility in requirements enables innovation

**3. Accessibility Matters**
- X+DS intentionally designed with fewer prerequisites
- Lower barriers increase diversity of students
- 850 students in X+DS (2 years) shows demand for accessible pathways

**4. Multi-Unit Governance Works**
- CS+X: 14 programs across 5 colleges demonstrates scalability
- X+DS: 3-unit partnership (CS, Statistics, iSchool) creates comprehensive foundation
- Clear governance structures essential for sustainability

**5. Maintain Core Rigor**
- CS+X students take same fundamental CS courses as CS majors
- Equivalent career preparation outcomes
- Interdisciplinary â  diluted

#### Enrollment & Growth Insights

**CS+X Enrollment Patterns:**
- 1,098 students across 14 programs (Fall 2022)
- CS+Economics: highest enrollment (156 students)
- Growth from 4 programs (2014) to 14 programs (2023)
- Acceptance rate: 18.5% (vs. <7% for traditional CS)

**X+DS Enrollment:**
- 850 students across 4 programs (launched 2023)
- Rapid uptake suggests strong market demand
- Aligns with 36% projected growth in data science jobs by 2031

**Interpretation:**
- Both models demonstrate sustained student interest
- Multiple pathways reduce bottlenecks in CS education
- Interdisciplinary programs attract students who might not pursue traditional CS

#### Career Outcomes Lessons

**CS+X Success Factors:**
- Graduates equally prepared for tech careers as traditional CS majors
- Unique interdisciplinary expertise valued by employers
- 9+ years of data validates model

**X+DS Career Preparation:**
- Strong industry demand for data science skills
- Career services provide targeted support
- Graduates enter finance, insurance, IT, professional services

**Recommendation:** Maintain strong career services integration for emerging AI programs

### B. Infrastructure & Partnership Lessons

#### Computing Infrastructure

**Investment Scale Matters:**
- Nearly $270 million in AI funding since 2019 enables top-5 national ranking
- DeltaAI triples AI computing capacity
- Dedicated AI infrastructure (HOLL-I with Cerebras CS-2) provides unique advantages

**Recommendation:** Secure dedicated AI computing resources early in program development

#### Research Institute Model

**Multi-Partner Institutes Work:**
- IBM-Illinois: $200M, 10-year commitment provides stability
- C3.ai DTI: Multi-university model enables larger scale
- Molecule Maker Lab: Multi-institutional team accelerates innovation

**Key Success Factors:**
- Long-term financial commitments (10-year, 5-year grants)
- Clear focus areas (hybrid cloud, quantum, materials, agriculture)
- Physical infrastructure (buildings, labs)
- Industry + academic + government partnerships

**Recommendation:** Pursue multi-year, multi-partner institute models for sustainability

#### Industry Partnership Approaches

**Effective Partnership Structures:**
- IBM: Building + research funding + long-term commitment
- Amazon: Research awards + fellowships + hiring pipeline
- Microsoft: Institute co-founding + research collaboration + hiring
- Google: PhD fellowships + research collaboration
- C3.ai: Research funding + employee education programs

**Lesson:** Best partnerships combine funding, student support, and workforce development
